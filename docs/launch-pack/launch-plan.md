**Version:** 1.0.0

## Preamble

This launch plan operationalises the go-to-market strategy described in the business plan across seven executable sections. Every tactic is stated with an owner, a cost, and a success metric. The plan is scoped for a solo founder (P5 Alex Kim) with zero paid advertising budget at V1 and a content-first, traffic-first commercial logic.

The plan is structured around three phases that mirror the commercial model's sequencing requirement: consumer traffic must be established before farm shop owner acquisition proceeds, and farm shop owner acquisition must demonstrate results before Bronze conversion is credible. No section of this plan violates that sequence.

**Compliance constraints that govern this plan:**
- PECR Regulation 6: marketing emails to farm shop owners require explicit opt-in consent (V2-C4). No bulk email to owner list before consent is recorded.
- UK GDPR / EU GDPR: all email collection requires lawful basis; opt-in checkbox in claim flow (V2-C4).
- OSA 2023: UGC (reviews, photos) must comply with illegal content obligations before F5/F4 go live.
- Natasha's Law: no marketing claims implying food safety certification — Farmmap is a discovery platform, not a food safety authority.
- CMA Green Claims Code: sustainability claims must be specific and substantiated. Do not claim "lowest carbon" without lifecycle data. "Shorter journeys from farm to table" is acceptable; "carbon neutral" is not.
- yourhonestybox.com data licence: 336 Irish/NI listings must not be marketed as live until written consent is confirmed (V1-C3). All marketing materials that reference Ireland/NI are conditional on this licence being executed.

## Section 1: Positioning and Messaging

### 1.1 Positioning Statement

**Geoffrey Moore template:**

> For UK and Irish adults who want to buy local food but cannot find where to buy it, Farmmap is the map-first farm shop and honesty box directory that makes every farm shop, gate stall, and roadside honesty box discoverable from a mobile phone — unlike BigBarn or Google Maps — because it is the only directory purpose-built for browsable local food discovery that covers honesty boxes as first-class listings across the whole of the UK and Ireland, with no account required and no subscription needed to use the map.

**Working tagline:** "Real farms. Real food. Find them."

**Short-form positioning (one sentence for press packs, headers):** "Britain and Ireland's farm shops and honesty boxes — all on one map."

---

### 1.2 Core Message Hierarchy

**Primary message:**
Farmmap is the only place to find every farm shop, honesty box, farm gate stall, and roadside produce stand near you — on a map, on your phone, in under 20 seconds, without creating an account.

**Supporting proof point 1 — Scale and density:**
Farmmap launches with 953 verified listings across England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland, and the Republic of Ireland, including over 260 honesty boxes that appear nowhere else online.

**Supporting proof point 2 — Honesty box uniqueness:**
Honesty boxes are first-class listings on Farmmap — an unstaffed roadside stall with fresh eggs or home-grown strawberries appears on Farmmap's map with the same prominence as a full farm shop. No other UK-wide directory has made this choice.

**Supporting proof point 3 — Trust and accuracy:**
Claimed listings carry a verified owner badge confirming the information is current. Farm shop owners can update their hours, upload photos, and flag temporary closures. If it is verified, it is reliable.

### 1.4 Brand Voice Guidelines

**What Farmmap sounds like:**
Farmmap sounds like a knowledgeable friend who genuinely knows where all the good farm shops are — warm, specific, and quietly confident. It speaks plainly and uses real place names, real distances, and real numbers. It does not use marketing language ("unlock the potential of", "supercharge your visibility") or corporate vagueness ("best-in-class", "seamless"). When it describes a farm shop, it sounds like someone who has been there.

**What Farmmap does not sound like:**
Farmmap does not sound like a tech startup (no "disrupt", "democratise", "platform", "ecosystem"). It does not sound like a food industry trade publication (no jargon, no regulatory citation in consumer-facing copy). It does not sound like a farm shop industry lobby (no romanticisation that erases the commercial reality of running a farm). It never makes food safety claims it cannot substantiate.

**Tone by context:** Informative for listing descriptions. Warm for consumer marketing. Direct and evidence-led for farm shop owner communications. Matter-of-fact for trade press.

### 3.2 Content Pillars

Four recurring content themes sustain the 90-day plan without requiring constant new ideation:

**Pillar 1 — Discovery (40% of content):**
"Did you know there's an honesty box in [village]?" / "Five farm shops within 20 miles of [city] you might not know about" / "Farmmap found [N] farm shops in [county] — here's one you'll want to visit."
Purpose: consumer acquisition. Shows the product working. Shareable. County-tagged for local reach.

**Pillar 2 — Behind the farm (30% of content):**
Short stories about specific farms and their owners. "The dairy farm in [county] that's been selling eggs at the gate for 40 years." "Meet the farm shop that started as a wheelbarrow of vegetables."
Purpose: trust and brand. Farmmap is not a tech product; it is the map layer in front of real places run by real people. This pillar makes that tangible. Source material: public information from claimed listing pages and farm shop websites; do not fabricate details.

**Pillar 3 — Seasonal and practical (20% of content):**
"Best farm shops for Christmas turkeys in the South West." / "Strawberry season — which farm shops have pick-your-own this July?" / "Back to school: farm shops with bread, cheese, and lunch box staples in Yorkshire."
Purpose: search-aligned, timely content that matches seasonal purchase intent. Instagram carousels perform well for seasonal roundups. Cross-post with county guide SEO articles.

**Pillar 4 — Honesty box spotlight (10% of content):**
Dedicated honesty box features — the product's most differentiated editorial territory. "The stall in a field in County Clare that sells nothing but garlic." "A complete guide to honesty boxes on the North Downs."
Purpose: media hook. This content is unique. No other account posts exclusively about UK and Irish honesty boxes. It establishes Farmmap's category ownership and creates PR-worthy content.

### 4.2 Story Angles

At least five distinct angles, each pitched separately to the appropriate publication:

**Angle 1 — The honesty box story (BBC Countryfile, Country Living, Guardian Weekend):**
"Britain has over 2,000 unstaffed roadside stalls — honesty boxes — selling fresh produce entirely on trust. Nobody has ever mapped them all. Until now."
This is the editorial hook that has genuine novelty. No publication has run this piece because no product made it possible. Farmmap makes it possible. Data-led: specific counts, specific counties, specific examples. Pitch with a map screenshot showing honesty box pin density.

**Angle 2 — The data accuracy problem (Which?, tech desk, consumer journalists):**
"43% of UK farm shops have incorrect or stale data on Google Maps (AHDB Farm Business Survey 2023). Farmmap launches with 953 verified listings. Here is why the accuracy problem matters to consumers who drove to a closed farm shop."
Data-led provocation. Appeals to consumer journalism. Positions Farmmap as a trust correction rather than a new directory.

**Angle 3 — The farm retail economics story (Farmers Weekly, The Grocer, trade press):**
"The Farm Retail Association calculates that 3.6 million UK adults have never visited a farm shop because they cannot find one. At average farm shop spend of £28 per visit, that is approximately £137 million in annual consumer spend not reaching farm shops — not because those shops lack the product, but because they are invisible to digital discovery."
Sector economics angle for trade publications. Positions Farmmap as economic infrastructure for the farm retail sector.

**Angle 4 — The ELM diversification timing story (farm business press, Farmers Guardian):**
"England's Basic Payment Scheme ends entirely in 2027. The NFU's Diversification Survey shows 38% of diversifying farms intend to open or expand farm retail. Farmmap launches at the peak of that wave — providing the discovery infrastructure that new farm shops need from day one."
Policy and timing angle. Appeals to agricultural business media covering the ELM transition.

**Angle 5 — The yourhonestybox.com partnership story (Irish media, NI media):**
"yourhonestybox.com has spent five years building the definitive community map of Ireland's honesty boxes. Farmmap extends that dataset to UK farm shops, making the full British-Irish farm direct food culture discoverable for the first time."
Partnership angle for Irish and NI media. Co-announcement with yourhonestybox.com; requires their confirmation before pitching (conditional on V1-C3). Position yourhonestybox.com prominently — this is their audience.

**Angle 6 — The founder story (food writing community, local food blogs):**
"One person. 953 farm shops. How Farmmap went from the observation that 2,310 UK farm shops have no web presence to a live map covering every known farm shop and honesty box in the UK and Ireland."
Human interest angle. Works for smaller publications and blogs. Generates backlinks from food community sites.

## Section 5: Community Growth

### 5.1 Farm Shop Owner Community Strategy

**Goal:** Build a community of claimed listing owners who are active on Farmmap, providing accurate data and generating the quality signals that make the product more valuable for consumers.

**Month 1–3:** Manually identify the 50 most photogenic, well-established farm shops in the beachhead counties (Surrey, North Yorkshire) from seed data. Check if they have Instagram or a website. Send a personalised "your listing is live" notification (not a marketing email — a factual notification) once sufficient traffic data exists. This is a human touchpoint from the founder, not an automated campaign.

**Owner onboarding sequence (automated, post-claim):**
1. Claim confirmed: welcome email with link to listing management.
2. Day 3: "Add a photo to your listing" — single-step instruction email.
3. Day 7: "Tell customers what you sell" — product category tags prompt.
4. Day 14: First analytics summary — "Here is what happened on your listing this week."

### 6.5 SEO Content Strategy — County Guide Articles

**Structure of a county guide article:**
Each article follows the same template. This enables faster production and helps with internal linking between articles.

Section 1: Introduction (150 words) — "The best farm shops in [County]: a guide by Farmmap"
Section 2: Featured listings (3–5 farms, each with 2-sentence description, product speciality, and link to their Farmmap listing page)
Section 3: "Also on the map" — list format of remaining county listings with name and location
Section 4: Honesty boxes in [County] (if listings exist) — specific section makes honesty box queries land on relevant articles
Section 5: How to find farm shops in [County] on your phone — one paragraph; reinforces the mobile use case; includes a screenshot of the map at county level
Section 6: FAQ — 3 questions targeting specific search queries: "Are there farm shops near [major town in county]?" / "What can I buy at farm shops in [County]?"

**Target word count:** 1,200–1,800 words (sufficient for long-form ranking without padding).

**Target queries per article:**
- Primary: "farm shops in [county]" / "farm shops [county] UK"
- Secondary: "best farm shops [county]" / "farm shops near [town in county]"
- Tertiary: "[county] honesty boxes" / "local food [county]"

**Cadence:** One article per fortnight for the first 24 months. Priority order: beachhead counties first (Surrey, North Yorkshire), then Devon, Herefordshire, then by FRA member density.

**Article production time:** 3–4 hrs per article (founder). At one per fortnight, this is 1.5–2 hrs/week average.

**Internal linking:** Every county guide links to: (a) the top 5 listing pages in that county (drives SEO equity to listing pages); (b) adjacent county guides as they are published; (c) the honesty box overview page (/honesty-boxes/) for counties with honesty box listings.

### 7.2 Rationale for Metric Targets

**Claimed listings:** The claim rate (15% at Month 12 = ~143 claimed listings) drives Bronze conversion. The 90-day target of 100 claimed listings is on the trajectory to that Month 12 target. 15 at Day 30 assumes only beachhead county active outreach in the first month.

**Unique consumers/month:** The 60,000 target at 180 days is below the 40,000 Month 6 target in the business plan, because Month 6 aligns with approximately Day 180 (six months post-launch). The targets are consistent. Consumer traffic is the load-bearing assumption of the entire model: without 40,000 monthly visitors by Month 6, the Bronze conversion funnel does not have sufficient top-of-funnel volume to reach 9 paying subscribers by Month 12.

**Bronze subscribers:** Zero at 30 days and zero at 60 days is intentional. The business plan's commercial logic is explicit: no Bronze conversion attempts before three months of traffic data on a listing. The first Bronze subscribers are realistic at Day 75–90 at the earliest, which explains the 3–5 target at 90 days (not Month 12).

**Instagram followers:** 3,000 at 180 days is a modest target consistent with organic-only growth in a niche category. The milestone that matters is not follower count but engagement rate and profile visits per post — which determine whether Instagram becomes a traffic source for farmmap.co.uk.

**FRA engagement status:** The progression from "email sent" to "meeting held" to "MOU" across 180 days reflects the realistic speed of trade body relationship development. The FRA's annual conference and show calendar (Balmoral Show in May is the first major opportunity) create natural touchpoints.

### 7.3 Traffic-to-Bronze Conversion Funnel

The following conversion funnel maps the journey from an anonymous Google search to a paying Bronze subscriber. All rates are targets; actual rates will be measured from Month 1.

```
Stage                                         Target Rate      Volume at Month 12
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Google organic impressions (Search Console)  —                1,000,000/month
↓  Click-through rate (impression → visit)   10% (target)
Google organic sessions (Plausible)          —                100,000/month
↓  Visit to listing detail page view         60%
Listing detail page views                    —                60,000/month
↓  Listings with 50+ views in 30 days        —                ~100 listings/month
  (triggers traffic-triggered email)
↓  Traffic-triggered email open rate         50%              ~50 opens
↓  Email to claim initiation                 30%              ~15 claim starts/month
↓  Claim initiation to completion            75%              ~11 completions/month
↓  Claim completion to Bronze conversion     6%               ~9 Bronze/year cumulative
```

**Target Bronze count at Month 12:** 9 paying subscribers (base case from business plan).

**Key sensitivities:**
- If email open rate drops to 30% (from 50%), Bronze count at Month 12 falls to ~5.
- If claim-to-Bronze conversion drops to 3% (sensitivity case), Month 12 Bronze count falls to ~4–5.
- FRA endorsement raises the conversion rate at "email to claim initiation" to approximately 90% (warm referral vs. cold email), potentially tripling the Month 12 Bronze count if active by Month 6.

**Recommended weekly tracking actions:**
1. Check Google Search Console for impressions, clicks, and average position on target queries.
2. Check Plausible for monthly unique visitors and bounce rate on listing pages.
3. Check admin console for new claim initiations and completions.
4. Check Stripe for new Bronze subscriptions (zero expected until Month 3).
5. Check Resend for traffic-triggered email delivery rate and open rate.

### 7.4 Success Gate Definitions

**30-day gate (minimum viable launch):**
At least 5,000 unique map sessions. At least 2 press mentions. At least 10 listings claimed organically (without founder outreach). Google Search Console shows impressions growing week-over-week.
If not met: investigate SEO indexation (sitemap submitted? structured data valid? Core Web Vitals passing?). Check whether the press release was received and whether embargo timing was appropriate.

**90-day gate (product-market fit signal):**
At least 30,000 unique monthly sessions. At least 100 claimed listings. At least 3 Bronze subscribers. At least one FRA meeting completed.
If not met at 90 days: investigate whether beachhead county outreach is generating claims; assess whether the traffic-triggered email is reaching owners who have valid email addresses in seed data; assess whether the Bronze conversion prompt is visible in the claim flow.

**180-day gate (commercial model validation):**
At least 60,000 unique monthly sessions. At least 9 Bronze subscribers (on-track for Month 12 target). At least one formal FRA meeting with positive indication of endorsement path. yourhonestybox.com data licence signed.
If not met at 180 days: revisit the beachhead county strategy; assess whether the SEO content programme requires acceleration (increase to one article per week); assess whether the FRA relationship requires escalation (attend a trade event in person; seek an FRA board member referral).
