# Farmmap — Saturation Index

## Scoring Framework

The saturation index scores the "local food directory" and "farm shop marketplace"
space on a 0–10 scale:

- **0–3 (Green):** Nascent or lightly occupied space. First-mover advantage is large;
  differentiation is easy because few incumbents exist.
- **4–6 (Amber):** Moderately occupied space. Differentiation is possible but requires
  a clear and defensible angle. Incumbents exist but have structural weaknesses.
- **7–9 (Red):** Saturated space. Differentiation argument is mandatory. Incumbent
  lock-in is strong. New entrant needs a compelling 10× improvement on a specific
  dimension.
- **10 (Black):** Commoditised. No room for a new entrant without an acquisition of
  an existing player.

A score above 7 requires an explicit differentiation argument per the task brief.

---

## Scoring Dimensions

### Dimension 1 — Number of Direct Competitors (UK + Ireland)

Active, general-purpose local food directories:
- BigBarn (active, UK, 24 years)
- FRA Directory (active, UK, trade body)
- yourhonestybox.com (active, Ireland/NI, honesty boxes only)
- LocalFoodFinder (inactive — ghost)

Active marketplaces:
- No active farm shop marketplace with Stripe Connect Standard model exists in the UK
  [assessment — Farmdrop closed 2022; no direct replacement at platform level;
  farm shops use Shopify or WooCommerce independently]

**Score contribution: 3/10** — Three active competitors with meaningful presence,
one inactive, zero direct marketplace competitors. Not saturated.

### Dimension 2 — Consumer Awareness / Market Maturity

- Consumers are not brand-loyal to any single local food directory. No equivalent
  of "I Rightmove'd it" exists for farm shops. Consumer behaviour is dominated by
  Google search and word-of-mouth, not a named directory platform.
- YouGov / FSA consumer research confirms 43% of UK adults value local food but
  no single directory reaches even 5% of this population with meaningful engagement
  [inference — BigBarn at 30,000–80,000 monthly sessions vs the 22.3 million UK
  local food shoppers = <0.4% penetration of the addressable consumer base].
- Market awareness of the directory category is low: most farm shop customers
  find shops through word of mouth, local signage, or Google Maps — not a dedicated
  local food directory.

**Score contribution: 2/10** — Consumer market is pre-awareness for the category.
No incumbent has achieved market-defining presence.

### Dimension 3 — Incumbent Quality / Defensibility

- **BigBarn:** Lowest-quality UX incumbent in the space. Desktop-first, ageing
  codebase, weak mobile experience. Cannot rapidly ship a modern mobile-first
  redesign without a full rebuild. Defensibility: LOW.
- **FRA Directory:** High institutional credibility but not a product competitor.
  Not designed to be consumer-first. Defensibility against a well-executed
  consumer product: MEDIUM — credibility advantage, but product cannot compete
  on UX.
- **yourhonestybox.com:** No commercial model, no development budget, Ireland/NI
  only. Defensibility: LOW.

**Score contribution: 2/10** — Incumbent quality is weak. No incumbent has the
resources or product quality to rapidly respond to a well-executed modern entrant.

### Dimension 4 — Category Differentiation Available

Gaps in the current competitive landscape that remain unoccupied:
1. **Honesty boxes as first-class listing type:** No UK directory does this. yourhonestybox.com
   does it in Ireland/NI but has no commercial model and no UK coverage.
2. **Map-first mobile experience:** BigBarn has a map component; it is not the
   primary interaction model. No incumbent has built the "browse the pins while
   driving" experience.
3. **Marketplace with Stripe Connect:** No farm shop directory currently offers
   a clean, compliant online ordering capability at the platform level. Farm shops
   use Shopify independently, not as part of a discovery platform.
4. **Unified UK + Ireland coverage:** No single directory covers England, Scotland,
   Wales, Northern Ireland, and Republic of Ireland in one map.
5. **Performance dashboard for shop owners:** No current directory provides
   shop-owners with data about who is viewing their listing and when.

These five gaps represent substantial differentiation headroom. The space is not
occupied in the way that, for example, UK property portals (Rightmove, Zoopla,
OnTheMarket) are occupied, where any new entrant faces established multi-billion-pound
competitors with near-complete inventory and consumer lock-in.

**Score contribution: 1/10** (low score = high differentiation available)

### Dimension 5 — Barriers to Entry for Future Competitors

- Building a local food directory with 1,000+ verified listings requires significant
  data acquisition effort. Farmmap's 953 seed listings represent a genuine head start
  that a new entrant in Month 13 would need to replicate.
- SEO domain authority takes 18–24 months to build for a new entrant targeting
  "near me" queries.
- Relationships with FRA and Bord Bia are relationship assets that take time to
  develop.
- However: barriers to entry are not high in absolute terms. A well-funded entrant
  (e.g., a media company, a supermarket, or a VC-backed startup) could replicate
  Farmmap's V1 in 6–9 months with £200,000–£500,000 of investment. The window for
  Farmmap to establish a defensible position is 12–18 months.

**Score contribution: 3/10** — Moderate. Barriers exist but are not prohibitive.
Time is the most important strategic variable.

## Verdict: Score = 2/10 — Low Saturation

The local food directory and farm shop marketplace space in the UK and Ireland is
lightly occupied, poorly served by existing incumbents, and structurally ready for
a well-executed modern entrant. A score of 2 does not trigger the mandatory
differentiation argument threshold (>7). However, the low saturation score does not
mean the market is easy — it means the competitive environment is not the primary
risk. Execution, consumer acquisition, and farm shop owner sales conversion are the
primary risks, not competitive pressure.

The saturation picture could change materially if:
1. BigBarn receives investment and rebuilds its product
2. FRA commissions a new map-first directory (potentially with BigBarn)
3. A VC-backed entrant arrives in the 12–18 month window before Farmmap achieves
   defensive consumer traffic scale
4. A large media company (e.g., The Guardian's Feast platform, BBC Good Food) builds
   a farm shop directory as a content vertical

None of these scenarios are currently probable in the near term, but scenario 3 is
plausible enough to warrant urgency of execution.

## Differentiation Argument (Advisory — Not Required at Score 2, but Provided)

Although the score is well below 7, the differentiation argument is documented here
because it is the core of every commercial conversation Farmmap will have:

**Farmmap's five-point differentiation from any existing or near-term competitor:**

1. **Honesty boxes first.** No competitor treats honesty boxes as a first-class
   listing type with their own search category. This is a unique, defensible, and
   immediately visible differentiation. Every user who successfully finds an honesty
   box via Farmmap becomes a brand advocate because Google Maps, BigBarn, and
   Facebook all fail them on this exact task.

2. **Mobile-first, map-primary.** The product is designed for the "driving through
   the countryside" use case. BigBarn's map is supplementary; Google Maps is not
   specialist. Farmmap's Core Web Vitals score and offline tile caching strategy
   (architecture phase to confirm) target rural mobile network conditions
   specifically.

3. **UK + Ireland in one place.** No competitor covers the five nations plus
   Republic of Ireland in a single consumer-facing map. This is Farmmap's entire
   addressable universe; for competitors it is an afterthought or an exclusion.

4. **Platform, not just directory.** Farmmap is the only local food discovery product
   planning a native marketplace layer (Silver/Gold) that keeps the farm shop as
   merchant of record. Farmdrop tried to be the retailer and failed. Shopify gives
   shops e-commerce but no discovery audience. Farmmap is the combination of
   audience and commerce that does not currently exist.

5. **Portfolio compounding.** No competitor has Farmmap's portfolio optionality.
   When Farmmap is demonstrably the best-executed map-first directory for one
   fragmented sector, the same product logic applies to seven others. This is an
   argument for long-term strategic value, not a consumer-facing differentiator —
   but it matters for investor conversations and for resisting acqui-hire approaches.
