## Key Findings

### Strengths: Durable core is the domain and the design

The eight Strengths confirm that Farmmap enters the market with genuine structural
advantages: an exact-match domain that compounds with age, 953 listings that give
the consumer map immediate density, a permanent free tier that removes the most common
adoption objection, and a map-first mobile-first UX that is demonstrably superior to
every existing UK farm shop directory. These are not easily replicated by incumbents
(BigBarn is entrenched but weak on mobile UX and modern design) or by a new entrant
without 12–18 months of lead time.

The multi-tenant architecture (S3) and the honesty box first-class listing type (S8)
are the two Strengths with the clearest moat character: the architecture creates a
portfolio multiplication option no single-vertical competitor has, and the honesty
box coverage creates territorial differentiation in Ireland and NI that no UK
competitor has yet prioritised.

### Weaknesses: Two structural, one pre-launch blocker

The eight Weaknesses divide into three categories:

**Pre-launch blocker (one item):** W6 (yourhonestybox.com copyright risk on 336 Irish
listings) is the only Weakness with a potential blocking character before launch. It
has a clear resolution path — permission, replacement, or launch without the
yourhonestybox.com subset — but must be resolved before V1 goes live. This is already
a carry-forward condition from the discovery decision.

**Structural product gaps (two items, long-duration):** W1 (no reviews layer) and
W2 (demand leakage with no ordering waitlist) are design decisions that can be
addressed in the V2 and V1 product specs respectively. W2 is low engineering cost
and should be prioritised. W1 requires roadmap commitment but is not a launch blocker.

**Execution and commercial risks (five items):** W3 (standalone revenue insufficient),
W4 (hard and expensive supply-side acquisition), W5 (attribution gap), W7 (premature
engine investment), and W8 (no FRA relationship) are all addressable through execution
discipline, go-to-market sequencing, and founder relationships. None is structural
in the sense of requiring a design change to the core product.

### Opportunities: O4 is the largest actionable TAM signal

The most immediately actionable opportunity is O4: 2,310 UK farm shops with no
website for whom a free Farmmap listing is their only digital presence. The correct
pitch frame is not "get listed on another directory" but "we are your Google presence."
Combined with O5 (ELM-driven farm shop diversification creating new operators who have
never needed digital discovery), the digitally-absent segment is large, motivated, and
approachable through FRA data.

O3 (yourhonestybox.com as a partnership or acquisition target) is the highest-value
single-action opportunity: it simultaneously resolves W6, neutralises T2 and T4, and
turns a competitor into a supply-side asset.

### Threats: No fundamental threat; one high-probability near-term risk

T1 (Google Maps farm shop category expansion) is the only systemic existential threat —
if Google adds a farm shop filter with enriched listings, it commoditises the core
consumer discovery proposition. The mitigation is to build Farmmap into the trust and
commerce layer (reviews, verified badges, ordering) that Google Maps cannot easily
replicate at the micro-producer level. This is a long-term strategic imperative, not
a near-term operational concern.

T4 (yourhonestybox.com database rights claim) is the highest-probability near-term
risk because it is a specific, foreseeable event that could occur within 30–90 days
of launch. It is addressed as a pre-launch blocker (see above).

T5 (farm shop digital fatigue) is the most persistent commercial threat — it affects
every Bronze sales conversation. The mitigation (SO strategy SO4, WT mitigation WT3)
is to generate consumer traffic evidence before any commercial outreach, so that the
sales conversation is grounded in data rather than promises.

No threat has been identified that represents a fundamental market failure, a
regulatory prohibition, or a competitive foreclosure that would prevent Farmmap from
building and operating its product. The Google threat (T1) is real but is not
imminent, and Farmmap's response — owning the commerce and trust layer — is available
and logical.

## Priority Actions Confirmed by SWOT

In order of urgency (updated to reflect yourhonestybox.com consent confirmation):

1. **Before V1 launch — UPDATED:** Formalise yourhonestybox.com partnership (O3, S4)
   — the legal risk (W6 / T4) is RESOLVED. The action is now partnership formalisation,
   not legal remediation. Recommended: data licence or co-branding agreement that
   acknowledges the existing consent relationship and converts it into a named
   structural partnership. "Powered in partnership with yourhonestybox.com" on Irish
   honesty box listings is a moat-building move, not a compliance fix.

2. **Before V1 launch:** Initiate FRA data-sharing and endorsement conversation
   (W8 / T3) — founder-level relationship, not a product dependency. This is now the
   highest-priority unresolved action before launch.

3. **In V1 product spec:** Add demand-capture waitlist mechanism (W2) — single
   email-capture field, negligible engineering cost, high V3 conversion value.

4. **In V2 product spec:** Design reviews and ratings system (W1) — lightweight star
   rating with moderation, removes permanent Google dependency for trust layer.

5. **Throughout growth phase:** Gate Bronze sales conversations on listing traffic
   threshold (W5 / T5) — evidence-first sales motion, not promises.
