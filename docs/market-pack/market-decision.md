# Farmmap — Market Viability Decision

## Gate: Market Viability (Phase 2)

**PROCEED.**

---

## Evidence Summary

### TAM

The total UK + Ireland local food direct-sales market (farm shops, farmers markets,
honesty boxes, farm gate) is approximately **£1.77 billion per annum** in consumer
spend. This figure is assembled from FRA benchmarking data (UK farm shops at ~£1.3bn),
FARMA census (UK farmers markets at ~£225m), DEFRA farm direct-sales survey (honesty
boxes at ~£72m), and Bord Bia data (Republic of Ireland at ~£180m). Every component
is cited with its source in tam-sam-som.md. The TAM is large enough to support
Farmmap at the revenue targets in the base case — even a 0.01% revenue capture
from TAM exceeds Year 1 targets.

### SAM

The serviceable addressable market is approximately **5,700 listings** with meaningful
digital discovery upside: 3,000 UK farm shops (net of 400 large operations that are
self-sufficient digitally), 2,000 UK honesty boxes and gate stalls, and 700 Ireland/NI
operations. At full Bronze penetration (20% of the serviceable listing universe),
annual subscription revenue potential is approximately £250,000–£500,000. This matches
the value-bench.md base-to-best scenario band.

### SOM

- **V1 (12 months):** 40,000–100,000 monthly visitors. Achievable with structured
  SEO programme; 100k is aspirational and requires a full content programme from
  launch. 40k is the realistic floor with standard directory SEO.
- **V2 (24 months from Bronze launch):** 100–150 paying Bronze subscribers;
  ~£24,000–£36,000/year. The 100-subscriber milestone is the proof-of-concept gate
  for engine generalisation per discovery-decision.md conditions.
- **V3 (36 months from V1 launch):** ~£164,000/year total revenue across Bronze,
  Silver, Gold, and add-ons — base case. Consistent with value-bench.md Year 3
  calculation.
- **V3 (60 months from V1 launch):** £286,000–£450,000/year single vertical.
  Portfolio at Year 5: ~£2.2m across all 7 verticals.

### Competitor Assessment

Eight competitors assessed. No competitor offers the full Farmmap proposition
(map-first, farm shops + honesty boxes, UK + Ireland, free browse + subscription
+ marketplace). The saturation index is **2/10** — well below the 7/10 threshold
requiring a mandatory differentiation argument. BigBarn is the most direct product
competitor; its 24-year technical debt is Farmmap's primary opportunity. The FRA
directory is the most important institutional relationship — partnership is essential,
competition is damaging. yourhonestybox.com *(amended 2026-05-16)* — the database
rights concern is RESOLVED: the 336 Irish listings were obtained with explicit
consent. yourhonestybox.com is now a confirmed potential partner, not a legal risk.
Partnership formalisation recommended before V1 launch. Farmdrop's closure is
evidence that the retailer model fails; Farmmap's platform model is structurally
differentiated.

### Pricing Assessment

- Bronze at £20/month: correct. Defensible on value delivery ratio. Recommended:
  hold price, offer 90-day trial.
- Silver at £60/month + 3% commission: correct for shops at £1,500+ GMV/month.
  Recommended: 3-month trial at Bronze price with marketplace enabled to accelerate
  upgrade conversion.
- Gold at £100/month + 5% commission: correct structure, commission at risk above
  £8k GMV/month. Recommended: model a commission cap or sliding scale above £8k.
- Campaign add-ons at £50/campaign: fair for done-for-you output.

### Key Trends

- Post-COVID local food interest: sustained at structurally elevated baseline (not
  peaked). Tailwind.
- Cost of living: farm shops are benefiting from "intentional spending" pattern —
  consumers visit less frequently but spend more per trip and care more about the
  experience. Tailwind for discovery value.
- ELM / direct payment wind-down: creates supply-side incentive for farm shop
  formation and expansion across England, Wales, and Scotland. Multi-year tailwind
  for Farmmap's addressable listing universe.
- Sustainability / food miles: deepening, values-based consumer trend. Farmmap's
  positioning is directly aligned. Tailwind.
- yourhonestybox.com: growing at ~20–25%/year in listings; no commercial model;
  no signs of UK expansion. Monitor but not an immediate commercial threat.

### Channel Analysis

- **Consumer:** SEO is the primary channel (near-zero marginal CAC, compounds with
  content). Social and PR are supporting channels for launch amplification.
- **Farm shop owners:** FRA partnership is the highest-leverage channel (near-zero
  monetary CAC, 2–3× conversion rate vs cold outreach). Traffic-triggered personalised
  email is the next priority (automatable, low cost). Rep visits are high-conversion
  but high-cost and should be reserved for warm leads in high-density areas.
- **CAC range:** £0 (FRA referral) to £250 (targeted rep visit on warm lead) for
  Bronze. The saleability critique's £120–£250 estimate is realistic for the blended
  CAC across channels if FRA partnership is secured.

## Conditions Carry-Forward to Phase 3

These conditions are inherited from the discovery-decision.md and extended with
market research findings:

1. **yourhonestybox.com consent** *(RESOLVED — amended 2026-05-16)* — The 336 Irish
   listings were obtained with explicit consent from yourhonestybox.com. The legal
   condition from the discovery phase is closed. The compliance squad must record the
   consent basis in writing before V1 launch. The action is now partnership
   formalisation, not legal remediation: approach yourhonestybox.com to establish a
   formal data licence or co-branding agreement that converts the existing consent
   relationship into a named structural partnership. yourhonestybox.com is now
   correctly classified as a confirmed potential partner, not a competitive threat.

2. **FRA partnership** — must be initiated before V1 launch. Market research
   confirms this is the single highest-leverage action for Bronze acquisition.
   Recommended position: FRA membership badge on listings, reciprocal data sharing,
   positioning as complementary to (not competitive with) FRA's own directory.

3. **SEO content programme** — must be specified in the Phase 2 launch plan with
   budget and editorial calendar. Trend analysis confirms "farm shops near me" and
   "honesty boxes near me" are the primary consumer acquisition queries. County guide
   articles and schema markup are the primary SEO outputs. This is not a marketing
   add-on; it is the load-bearing mechanism for the 100k visitor target.

4. **Gold commission cap** — pricing hypothesis recommends designing a commission
   cap or sliding scale for Gold shops above £8,000/month GMV before V3 build.
   This should be in the commercial model specification for the architecture phase.

5. **Bronze trial offer** — pricing hypothesis recommends a 90-day trial mechanism
   for Bronze rather than discounting. This should be in the V2 commercial design.

## Decision Rationale

Market viability is confirmed on all five dimensions: the TAM is large (£1.77bn),
the SAM is accessible (5,700 listings, £250–500k annual revenue potential), the SOM
is realistic on the base case (£164k Year 3), the competitive landscape is lightly
occupied (saturation index 2/10), and the pricing structure is correctly benchmarked.

Trend analysis provides three independent structural tailwinds: sustained post-COVID
local food behaviour change, cost-of-living "intentional spending" pattern, and ELM
policy creating supply-side incentive for farm shop formation. No trend is working
against the proposition.

The yourhonestybox.com data rights issue *(amended 2026-05-16)* is RESOLVED —
the 336 Irish listings were obtained with explicit consent. There are no remaining
items with blocking potential on the market viability verdict. The UK market is the
primary SAM and is confirmed unaffected. The Irish market is now strengthened by the
confirmed consent relationship and the partnership formalisation opportunity it opens.

**Market viability is confirmed. Proceed to Phase 3.**
