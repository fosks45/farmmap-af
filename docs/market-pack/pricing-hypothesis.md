# Farmmap — Pricing Hypothesis

## Summary Verdict

- **Bronze at £20/month:** Price is correctly positioned. Not too cheap to be
  dismissed, not so expensive as to cause resistance from cautious small operators.
  Defensible on value if consumer traffic is established. Risk: still the hardest
  conversion given Facebook/Google-is-free objection.
- **Silver at £60/month + 3% commission:** Structurally correct. Price is right
  relative to Shopify comparables but requires £1,500/month GMV to justify the
  upgrade from Bronze. This is a natural inflection point, not a stretch.
- **Gold at £100/month + 5% commission:** Appropriately positioned for the
  high-commitment, high-revenue shop segment. Marketing services bundle justifies
  the premium over Silver. Risk: 5% commission is high once GMV is large; a
  high-GMV Gold shop will model the math and consider alternatives.
- **3% commission threshold at £20 minimum order:** Threshold is correctly set
  for the farm shop average basket but creates a small absolute fee problem at
  the minimum. Recommend keeping the threshold but acknowledging the edge case.

## 1. Bronze Tier — £20/month (£240/year)

### The Comparison Set

| Product | Price | What it delivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Business Profile** | Free | Search and Maps listing, photos, posts, reviews, messaging |
| **Facebook Business Page** | Free | Social posts, photos, events, messaging, follower engagement |
| **Yell.com** | £50–£200+/month [fact — Yell.com pricing pages, 2025; range reflects basic to premium subscription] | Business listing + advertising; claimed Yellow Pages domain authority |
| **Checkatrade** | £39–£72/month [fact — Checkatrade.com pricing pages, 2025; range for basic membership with lead generation focus] | Tradespeople directory; reviews; lead generation |
| **FRA membership** | £200–£800/year [inference — FRA membership tier descriptions] | Trade body membership, directory listing, events, technical support |
| **BigBarn premium** | ~£10–£30/month [inference — no published pricing; estimate from comparable directory models] | Directory listing, basic analytics |
| **Farmmap Bronze** | £20/month | Branded shop page, product catalogue, photos, dashboard, verified badge, enquiry form, newsletter/social add-ons available |

### Assessment

**Is £20/month the right price?**

Yes, with conditions.

**The Google and Facebook objection** (covered in saleability-critique.md) is the
hardest sales barrier, not the price point. Both are free and both deliver real
value to the farm shop owner's existing audience. Farmmap Bronze's value proposition
is not "better than Google/Facebook" — it is "reaching consumers that Google/Facebook
do not show your listing to." This is a new-customer acquisition argument, not a
presence management argument.

At £20/month:
- The subscription is below the "hesitation threshold" for most small business
  owners. Impulse decisions to subscribe are more likely at £20 than at £30 or £50.
  [inference — small business pricing psychology; subscription products below £25/month
  show significantly lower buyer hesitation in A/B test literature for SMB SaaS]
- The annual cost (£240) is defensible against the value delivery ratio from
  value-bench.md: even in the worst case, 3 new customers/year generating £600 in
  gross profit exceeds the Bronze cost by 2.5×. This is the sales argument, not the
  price reduction.
- At £20, Farmmap is positioned below Yell (£50+) and Checkatrade (£39+) — both of
  which have poor reputations for small rural business ROI. Farm shop owners who have
  been burned by Yell will perceive £20/month as a low-risk experiment rather than
  a commitment.

**Comparison to BigBarn:**
If BigBarn charges approximately £10–£30/month for a premium listing, Farmmap at £20
is within BigBarn's range. Farmmap must clearly demonstrate product superiority in
the sales conversation — the modern UX, the performance dashboard, and the verified
badge are the price justifiers. The Bronze pitch should lead with traffic data (once
available), not feature lists.

**Recommendation:** Hold £20/month. Do not discount. A free 90-day trial may be
more effective than a permanent price reduction for overcoming initial resistance.
The value-delivery ratio makes £20/month defensible; discounting signals lack of
confidence in the product.

### Bronze Commission on Campaign Add-ons (£50/campaign)

Benchmarks:
- Mailchimp: £8–£45/month for comparable email lists [fact — Mailchimp pricing 2025]
- Hootsuite (social): £49/month basic [fact — Hootsuite pricing 2025]

A one-off £50/campaign for a newsletter feature or social post is a reasonable
impulse add-on. This is not a recurring commitment — it is "pay to be featured this
week." The comparison to Mailchimp and Hootsuite is misleading because those are
tools the owner operates themselves. Farmmap's £50 is a done-for-you output,
which justifies the premium over tool subscription pricing.

## 2. Silver Tier — £60/month + 3% commission over £20

### The Comparison Set

| Product | Price | What it delivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Shopify Basic** | £25/month [fact — Shopify UK pricing 2025] | Full e-commerce store; no audience; 2.9%+30p per transaction (or less with Shopify Payments) |
| **Shopify Starter** | £5/month (links only; no store) | Order management via links; minimal |
| **Etsy** | 0.2p listing + 6.5% transaction + 4%+20p payment [fact — Etsy fee structure 2025] | Marketplace audience; no local food specificity; high fee stack for craft/artisan sellers |
| **Not On The High Street** | 25% commission [fact — NOTHS seller terms 2025] | Curated UK artisan marketplace audience; high commission but premium positioning |
| **WooCommerce** | Free plugin + hosting (~£10–£20/month) | E-commerce on own WordPress site; no audience; tech overhead |
| **Amazon Handmade** | 15% commission [fact — Amazon seller fees 2025] | Mass marketplace audience; poor fit for local food and farm identity |
| **Farmmap Silver** | £60/month + 3% over £20 | Farmmap audience + marketplace; shop is merchant of record; local food context |

### Is £60/month + 3% the right structure?

**Monthly fee assessment:**
The fee delta between Bronze and Silver is £40/month. The questions are: (1) does
the farm shop owner believe Farmmap's marketplace audience is worth £40/month more
than the Bronze dashboard? And (2) does the 3% commission create a meaningful
additional cost relative to alternatives?

For question (1): Shopify Basic at £25/month provides the full e-commerce stack but
zero audience. A farm shop owner considering Shopify Basic must then solve consumer
acquisition themselves (Google Ads, social media, word of mouth). Farmmap Silver at
£60/month bundles the audience acquisition problem into the subscription. For any shop
that would spend more than £35/month on digital marketing to drive online order traffic
(a low bar — one Facebook ad campaign/month at £40 would clear this threshold),
Silver is cheaper than Shopify + marketing.

For question (2): Shopify Payments charges approximately 2.9% + 30p per transaction.
Farmmap's 3% commission is in the same band. The 30p minimum-per-transaction on
Shopify is equivalent to 1.5% on a £20 basket. Farmmap's 3% with no per-transaction
fixed fee is comparable at the £20 basket level and slightly worse at very small
baskets. At larger baskets (£50+), Farmmap's 3% is slightly more expensive than
Shopify's percentage component but still materially cheaper than Etsy (6.5%) or
NOTHS (25%).

**The critical £1,500/month GMV threshold:**
Value-bench.md established that a shop needs approximately £1,500/month in Farmmap-
processed GMV before Silver unambiguously beats the cost of Bronze + their own
Shopify. At £1,500/month:
- Silver fee: £60
- Silver commission: £1,500 × 3% = £45
- Total Silver cost: £105/month

vs.
- Bronze fee: £20
- Shopify Basic: £25
- Shopify transaction fee: £1,500 × 2.9% + (number of transactions × £0.30)
  (if 30 orders/month at £50 avg: 30 × £0.30 = £9; percentage: £43.50 → total: £52.50)
- Total Bronze + Shopify cost: £20 + £25 + £52.50 = £97.50/month

At £1,500/month GMV, Silver (£105) vs Bronze+Shopify (£97.50) is nearly equivalent.
The upgrade to Silver only becomes clearly superior when the Farmmap audience
contribution to GMV is factored in — the farm shop does not need to find its own
Shopify customers. If Farmmap drives even 50% of that £1,500 GMV, Silver is clearly
better economics.

**Recommendation:** Maintain £60/month + 3%. The pricing is correctly calibrated
for shops generating £1,500–£3,000/month in online GMV. Consider introducing a
Silver trial (e.g., first 3 months at Bronze price with marketplace enabled) to
allow shops to validate the GMV potential before committing to the full Silver rate.

## 3. Gold Tier — £100/month + 5% commission over £30

### The Comparison Set

| Product | Price | What it delivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Shopify Advanced** | £289/month [fact — Shopify UK pricing 2025] | Full e-commerce with better rates (0.5% + lower per-transaction); no audience |
| **BigCommerce Standard** | £26/month scaling to £79/month [fact — BigCommerce UK 2025] | E-commerce platform; no audience |
| **Social media agency (basic)** | £500–£1,500/month [inference — typical UK small business social media retainer; no public benchmark; range from agency rate cards] | Managed social media; no directory listing; no marketplace |
| **PR/content marketing agency** | £1,000–£5,000/month [inference — UK regional food PR agencies; no public data] | Editorial coverage; brand building; no transactional capability |
| **Farmmap Gold** | £100/month + 5% over £30 | Silver + newsletter feature, weekly social posts, homepage placement, blog post, priority search, up to 1,000 products |

### Is £100/month + 5% the right structure?

**Monthly fee assessment:**
Gold at £100/month positions as a "done-for-you marketing package" rather than just
a directory listing. The nearest comparable is a basic social media management retainer
(£500–£1,500/month). On that comparison, £100 is a bargain — but it requires
Farmmap to actually deliver the newsletter features, social posts, and blog post
reliably. Gold pricing is contingent on operational delivery of marketing services,
which is a human-cost function for Farmmap.

**Commission structure: 5% over £30 minimum**
The £30 minimum order for Gold (vs £20 for Silver) reflects the expectation that Gold
shops have larger average baskets (they are larger, more established operations with
up to 1,000 SKUs). The 5% commission rate is higher than Silver (3%) — this is the
rate at which Gold becomes expensive for high-GMV shops.

Example: A Gold shop generating £8,000/month GMV pays:
- Monthly fee: £100
- Commission: £8,000 × 5% = £400
- Total: £500/month

At £8,000/month GMV, a farm shop considering Shopify Advanced (£289/month + 0.5%
transaction = £289 + £40 = £329/month, plus their own marketing costs) might
calculate that Gold is more expensive in direct cost — but only if they can generate
£8,000/month in Shopify orders without Farmmap's audience. The marketing services
bundle (newsletter, social, homepage placement) replaces a spend that would otherwise
run to £500+/month from an agency.

**Risk at high GMV:**
A Gold shop generating £15,000+/month will notice that the 5% commission becomes
a meaningful P&L line (£750/month on £15,000 GMV). At that point, Shopify Advanced
+ a separate marketing agency may become cheaper than Gold. Farmmap should consider
a commission cap or a sliding scale for Gold above a GMV threshold — e.g., 5% up to
£10,000 GMV/month, 3% above that. This protects retention of high-value shops.

**Recommendation:** Hold £100/month as the fee. Revisit the 5% commission for shops
above £8,000/month GMV. Consider a Gold Plus tier or a commercial negotiation floor
for shops exceeding £10,000/month GMV.

## 4. The 3% Commission Threshold Analysis

### Current structure: 3% commission on the total basket when order value exceeds £20

**Why £20 minimum?**
The £20 minimum is designed to avoid commission on trivial orders while capturing
meaningful ones. A farm shop selling a single item (one pint of milk, £1.50) should
not have a platform commission applied — the economics would be absurd and the UX
friction of a £0.045 commission would be confusing.

**What happens at small orders?**

| Order Value | Commission Paid | Effective Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| £5 | £0 | 0% | Below threshold — no commission |
| £15 | £0 | 0% | Below threshold — no commission |
| £20 | £0.60 | 3% | At threshold — commission applies |
| £25 | £0.75 | 3% | Above threshold |
| £50 | £1.50 | 3% | Standard basket |
| £100 | £3.00 | 3% | Large basket |

**Assessment of the threshold:**
- The £20 threshold is reasonable for the farm shop average basket (£18–£32 per
  FRA Retail Survey 2022/23). The commission begins to apply at approximately the
  median transaction value, meaning roughly half of orders will be commission-free
  and half will carry commission.
- This design choice reduces Farmmap's commission income materially from what a
  "commission on all orders" model would generate, but it solves the micro-transaction
  UX problem and makes the Silver proposition simpler to explain.
- Risk: a farm shop owner who primarily sells high-volume, low-value items (e.g.,
  eggs at £2.50/dozen, small jars of jam at £4) may find most of their orders are
  below £20, making Silver's commission mechanism irrelevant and reducing the
  incremental value of Silver vs Bronze.

**Recommendation:**
The £20 threshold is correctly set for the typical farm shop basket. Consider a
clarifying communication: "Commission applies to orders over £20 — no commission
on small orders or samples." This framing makes the threshold a feature (protection
from micro-transaction fees) rather than a limitation.

For Silver, an alternative framing: commission applies to the full basket value when
the basket exceeds £20 (not commission only on the amount above £20). Both
interpretations are possible from the intake; the "full basket" interpretation
generates more revenue and is standard for most marketplace commission models
(e.g., Etsy, Amazon). This should be confirmed in the build specification.

## 5. Summary Pricing Assessment

| Tier | Price | Verdict | Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bronze | £20/month | Correctly priced | Facebook/Google objection makes conversion hard regardless of price | Hold price; offer 90-day trial rather than discounting |
| Silver fee | £60/month | Correctly priced vs Shopify | Upgrade compelling at £1,500+ GMV; may be slow to convert | Consider 3-month trial at Bronze price with marketplace enabled |
| Silver commission | 3% over £20 | Correctly structured | Commission on full basket vs above-threshold needs clarification | Confirm interpretation in build spec |
| Gold fee | £100/month | Correctly priced vs agency alternatives | Requires reliable delivery of marketing services | Hold price; build SLA for marketing services delivery |
| Gold commission | 5% over £30 | At risk for high-GMV shops above £10k/month | High-GMV shops may calculate escape to Shopify | Consider commission cap above £8k GMV threshold |
| Campaign add-ons (Bronze) | £50/campaign | Fair for done-for-you output | Must deliver visible, measurable campaign | Hold price; include performance report per campaign |
