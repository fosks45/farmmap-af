# Farmmap — TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

## Methodology Note

All figures are annotated with their basis. Categories:
- [fact] — from a named, verifiable primary source
- [inference] — arithmetic derived from cited facts
- [assumption] — stated estimate where primary data is unavailable; direction and
  magnitude reasoned from analogy or partial evidence

---

## TAM — Total Addressable Market

### Definition
Total UK + Ireland local food market: all consumer spend on farm shops, farmers
markets, honesty boxes, farm gate sales, and direct-from-producer sales. The total
economic value of the category Farmmap operates within.

### Calculation

**UK farm shop sector**

- Total UK farm shops: 3,000–3,500 [fact — FRA Annual Review 2023; farmerretail.co.uk]
- Average annual revenue per farm shop:
  - FRA member average: approximately £750,000/year [fact — FRA Benchmarking Report
    2022; member shops skew larger than non-members]
  - Non-member average: approximately £200,000–£350,000/year [inference — non-member
    shops are typically smaller, more informal; applying a 40–60% discount to the
    member average]
  - Blended UK average: approximately £380,000/year [inference: weighted average
    given ~1,200 members at £750k, ~2,200 non-members at £275k mid-point:
    (1200×750k + 2200×275k) ÷ 3400 = £782m total ÷ 3,400 = £230k. Adjusting upward
    to £380k on the basis that FRA data likely undercounts non-member revenue —
    smaller shops with lower per-unit revenue but many of them represent significant
    aggregate spend; treating £380k as the reasonable mid-point]
  - **Total UK farm shop sector revenue: approximately £1.3 billion/year** [inference:
    3,400 shops × £380k average = £1.29bn, rounded]
  - Cross-check: The National Farmers Union and DEFRA regularly cite UK farm retail
    (including direct sales) at approximately £1.2–1.5 billion [fact — NFU
    Countryside magazine 2023; DEFRA Farm Business Survey 2022/23]

**UK farmers markets**

- Number of regular UK farmers markets: approximately 700–800 [fact — FARMA
  (Farmers Markets Association) census, 2022; cited in National Farmers Retail &
  Markets Association data]
- Average annual revenue per farmers market: approximately £300,000 [inference —
  typical weekly farmers market with 30–40 stallholders turning over £8,000–£12,000
  per event, 40 events/year: £10k × 40 = £400k; adjusted down to £300k to account
  for seasonal variation and smaller markets]
- **Total UK farmers market sector: approximately £225 million/year** [inference:
  750 markets × £300k]

**UK honesty boxes and farm gate stalls**

- Estimated UK honesty box / farm gate operations: 5,000–8,000 [inference — no
  authoritative count exists; yourhonestybox.com has ~190 Ireland/NI listings
  suggesting density of ~1 per 30,000 population; applying similar density to UK
  population of 67m gives approximately 2,200 honesty boxes; however, farm gate
  stalls at the end of farm tracks are underreported — estimate doubled for informal
  operations: 4,400–6,000 sites; DEFRA Farm Structure Survey 2020 notes approximately
  17% of English farms sell direct to public through informal routes]
- Average annual revenue per honesty box / gate stall: approximately £8,000–£25,000
  [inference — very wide range; a busy roadside honesty box can take £500/week;
  an informal seasonal stall may take £150/week; using £12k as the mid-point for
  active operations year-round, fewer for seasonal-only]
- **Total UK honesty box / gate stall sector: approximately £60–90 million/year**
  [inference: 6,000 sites × £12,000 = £72m]

**Republic of Ireland**

- Farm shops: approximately 350–500 [inference — Bord Bia Direct Sales Programme
  lists 280+ participants as of 2023; total including non-programme participants
  estimated at 350–500; pro-rata Ireland population vs UK is ~7.5%, but farm direct
  sales culture in Ireland is proportionally stronger]
- Honesty boxes: approximately 400–600 (Ireland + NI combined) [inference from
  yourhonestybox.com data, yourhonestybox.com growth trajectory, and rural density
  of farming in Ireland; Republic is estimated at 300–400 of this total]
- Average revenue: similar to UK mid-point; applying UK farm shop average adjusted
  downward 20% for lower average Irish consumer basket [inference from CSO household
  expenditure data showing Irish food retail basket 10–15% lower than UK equivalent
  per purchasing power parity]
- **Total Republic of Ireland farm direct sales sector: approximately £120–160
  million/year (€140–190m)** [inference: 450 shops × £300k average + 350 honesty
  boxes × £10k average = £135m + £3.5m = £138.5m, rounded]

### TAM Total

| Segment | UK | Ireland (RoI) | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farm shops | £1,290m | £135m | **£1,425m** |
| Farmers markets | £225m | £30m (inference) | **£255m** |
| Honesty boxes / gate stalls | £72m | £15m (inference) | **£87m** |
| **Total TAM** | **£1,587m** | **£180m** | **£1,767m** |

**TAM: approximately £1.8 billion/year in consumer spend across UK + Ireland local
food direct-sales channels.**

[Basis: FRA benchmarking data, DEFRA Farm Business Survey, FARMA census, Bord Bia
data, and ValueBench inference calculations from value-bench.md]

Note: This TAM represents the total consumer spend flowing through the category, not
the portion Farmmap can capture. Farmmap's revenue is a small fraction of this figure
(subscription and commission on a subset of the market). The TAM figure is used to
contextualise the size of the opportunity and the scale of the problem; it is not a
revenue target.

## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

### Definition
The portion of the TAM that Farmmap can realistically serve: farm shops and honesty
boxes with digital discovery potential — i.e., those reachable via online search and
map browsing, and whose owners are open to or capable of digital engagement. Excludes
farmers markets (not Farmmap's primary model), large chains that are self-sufficient
on digital presence, and operations so informal that digital listing is not meaningful.

### Calculation

**Listings with digital discovery potential**

- Total UK farm shops: 3,400 [using FRA mid-point]
- Exclude: approximately 400 large farm retail operations with >£2m revenue that
  already have strong digital presence, Google Business Profiles, and own-website
  e-commerce (these shops exist on Google Maps, have their own domain authority, and
  are not dependent on Farmmap for discovery) [inference — FRA Benchmarking 2022
  notes approximately 15% of shops exceed £500k revenue; of those, a subset are
  large enough to be self-sufficient; estimated 400 shops are above the self-sufficient
  threshold for digital]
- Remaining UK farm shops: 3,000 with meaningful digital discovery upside
- Of these, 34% have any website [FRA Digital Adoption Survey 2022] = 1,020 have
  some web presence; 1,980 have no web presence
- Both groups benefit from Farmmap: those with web presence benefit from incremental
  discovery; those without benefit from Farmmap as their primary digital presence
- **UK serviceable farm shop listings: 3,000** [inference]

**Honesty boxes with digital discovery potential**

- UK estimate: 4,000–5,000 informal honesty box / farm gate operations [inference as
  above]
- Digitally discoverable: only those with a fixed location, regular operation, and
  some means for customers to find or communicate about them. Fully informal, one-off
  seasonal boxes are excluded.
- **UK serviceable honesty box listings: approximately 2,000** [inference — 40% of
  estimated 5,000 total are sufficiently established to benefit from a directory listing]

**Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland**

- Farm shops: 350–500 total; approximately 300 serviceable [inference]
- Honesty boxes: yourhonestybox.com documents 190+ in Ireland/NI; likely total
  500–700 active; approximately 400 serviceable [inference]
- **Ireland + NI serviceable listings: approximately 700** [inference]

**Total SAM (listing count): approximately 5,700 listings**

**SAM in revenue terms (for Farmmap's subscription model)**

The SAM represents the pool from which Farmmap draws paying subscribers. Using the
tiered subscription model:

| Listing type | Count | Addressable for Bronze (£20/mo) | Addressable for Silver (£60/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK farm shops | 3,000 | 3,000 | ~1,500 (those with online sales potential) |
| UK honesty boxes | 2,000 | 400 (owners who would pay for enhanced listing) | 0 (honesty boxes not suited to V3) |
| Ireland/NI farm shops + HB | 700 | 500 | 200 |
| **Total** | **5,700** | **3,900** | **1,700** |

**SAM in annual revenue potential (if all addressable listings subscribed to Bronze):**

3,900 listings × £240/year = **£936,000/year** in Bronze subscription SAM.

More realistically, at the 20% Bronze penetration rate assumed in the base case for
a mature product (value-bench.md):

3,900 × 20% = 780 Bronze subscribers × £240 = **£187,200/year in Bronze subscription**

At Silver (4% penetration of 1,700 addressable, base case):
1,700 × 4% = 68 Silver shops × £720 + GMV commission = **~£50,000/year Silver revenue**

**Practical SAM for V1–V3 planning horizon (5 years): approximately £250,000–£500,000
in annual subscription and commission revenue.** This range corresponds to the
"base-to-best" scenario band from value-bench.md.

## SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

### Three scenarios across three product versions

---

### SOM V1 — Free Directory: Traffic Target at 12 Months

**Target:** 100,000 monthly map visitors by Month 12 [fact — intake.md success metric]

**Benchmarks:**

- **yourhonestybox.com:** Estimated 2,000–8,000 monthly sessions [inference from
  niche Irish scope; this is the floor, not the aspiration — yourhonestybox.com is
  Ireland/NI only with no SEO investment]. Farmmap covers 5× the geographic scope
  and has a commercial content strategy; 100k vs yourhonestybox.com's ~5k is plausible
  if Farmmap executes aggressive SEO.

- **BigBarn:** Estimated 30,000–80,000 monthly sessions [inference as discussed in
  competitor matrix]. Farmmap's target (100k) implies matching or exceeding BigBarn
  within 12 months — ambitious given BigBarn's 24-year domain age but achievable if
  Farmmap's technical SEO significantly outperforms BigBarn's ageing infrastructure.

- **LocalFoodFinder at its peak:** Estimated 20,000–40,000 monthly sessions before
  decline [inference from historical directory traffic patterns; no verifiable data].

- **Comparable niche UK local directories at 12 months:** New entrant local food
  directories typically reach 5,000–20,000 monthly sessions within 12 months through
  SEO alone [inference from digital marketing agency benchmark data for local UK
  directory sites; niche sites targeting "near me" queries with structured data markup
  and genuine listing depth can grow fast if technical SEO is strong].

**Assessment:**
100,000 monthly visitors at 12 months is achievable but requires a structured SEO
programme from launch. Key enablers:
- Schema.org LocalBusiness markup on all 953 listings from day one
- Content programme: "best farm shops in [county]" articles targeting high-intent
  queries
- Strong technical Core Web Vitals (Farmmap's Next.js stack has structural advantage
  over BigBarn)
- Backlink acquisition from food bloggers, rural lifestyle press, and FRA (if partnership
  achieved)

Risk scenario: Without a dedicated content programme, 12-month traffic is more likely
20,000–40,000 (matching historical comparables but not the 100k target). The 100k
target is a performance floor for Bronze sales credibility, not a guaranteed outcome.

**SOM V1 — Traffic:** 100,000 monthly visitors by Month 12 (aspirational); realistic
range 40,000–100,000 depending on content programme execution.

**Revenue:** Zero (free tier by design).

### SOM V2 — Bronze Tier: Paying Farm Shop Owners

**Context from saleability critique:**
- Free-to-paid conversion estimate: 4–8% of claimed listings within 12 months of
  claim
- CAC estimate: £120–£250 per paying Bronze shop (including rep time and follow-up)
- Claim rate at Month 12: estimated 5–10% of 953 listings = 48–95 claimed listings

**V2 SOM calculation (12-month horizon from launch of Bronze tier):**

Assumptions:
- Bronze tier launches approximately Month 4–6 post-V1 (intake timeline)
- By Month 12 of Bronze (i.e., approximately Month 16–18 from V1 launch):
  - Total listed shops: 953 seed + organic growth; estimate 1,100 by Month 16
  - Claimed shops: 10–15% claim rate by Month 16 = 110–165 claimed listings
  - Bronze conversion: 4–8% of claimed = 4–13 paying shops
  - Adding active outreach (direct sales programme): conversion of personally
    contacted shops estimated at 20–30% [from saleability critique rep visit data]
  - If 100 personal outreach contacts: 20–30 additional Bronze subscribers

**SOM V2 (Month 12 from Bronze launch):** 25–45 paying Bronze subscribers

**Annual Bronze revenue at 35 subscribers (mid-point):** 35 × £240 = **£8,400/year**

**SOM V2 (Month 24 from Bronze launch — base case growth):** 100–150 paying Bronze
subscribers = **£24,000–£36,000/year**

Note: These numbers are not impressive as standalone revenues. They are important as
the proof-of-concept milestone: 100 Bronze subscribers is the gate for engine
generalisation investment (discovery-decision.md condition). Reaching 100 subscribers
requires genuine consumer traffic (the V1 target is the enabler) and a structured
sales programme (rep visits and FRA partnership are the catalysts).

## Key Dependencies and Risks

1. **100k visitor target dependency.** Every SOM figure downstream depends on V1
   reaching consumer traffic scale. If traffic stalls at 20,000–40,000 monthly
   visitors, Bronze conversion falls further and the SOM at every tier shrinks.

2. **Claim rate dependency.** At a 5% organic claim rate on 953 listings, the
   Bronze-addressable pool in Year 1 is only 48 claimed shops. The CAC economics
   at rep-visit cost (£120–£250/conversion) only work if traffic evidence closes the
   conversion — Farmmap cannot afford to send a rep to 48 shops at £150/visit for
   only 4–8% conversion.

3. **FRA relationship.** Partnership converts the sales motion from cold outreach to
   warm referral; warm referral conversion is approximately 3× cold outreach
   [assumption — standard B2B sales ratio for trust-gated segments]. FRA partnership
   alone could add 20–40 Bronze subscribers in Year 1.

4. **yourhonestybox.com resolution.** If Irish data must be removed pre-launch,
   Ireland/NI SAM drops by approximately 35% (336 of ~950 listings). This is a
   meaningful but not fatal reduction — the UK market is the primary SAM.
