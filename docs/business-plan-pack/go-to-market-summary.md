# Farmmap — Go-to-Market Summary

## Overview

Farmmap's go-to-market strategy is a four-phase programme sequenced around a single
load-bearing insight from the viability gate: consumer traffic is the prerequisite
for every other commercial outcome. Without traffic, the Bronze pitch is an assertion;
with traffic, it is evidence. Every acquisition pound in Phase 1 and Phase 2 is spent
building traffic, not selling subscriptions.

---

## Phase 1 — Consumer SEO + PR Anchor (Months 1–6)

**Objective:** Establish Farmmap as the authoritative directory for farm shops and
honesty boxes in UK and Irish search results. Reach 40,000 monthly visitors by
Month 6.

**The PR angle:** "Britain's honesty boxes mapped for the first time." No authoritative,
browsable map of UK honesty boxes has ever existed. This is a genuine story — one that
appeals to rural affairs correspondents at The Guardian, The Times, BBC Country File,
Countryfile Magazine, Farmers Weekly, and Delicious Magazine. A single piece in a
national outlet with a backlink to the site is worth months of SEO content in
domain authority terms.

The yourhonestybox.com partnership announcement is the centrepiece of the PR push.
"The first UK+Ireland honesty box map, built in partnership with yourhonestybox.com"
is a credibility claim backed by an existing community relationship. It pre-empts the
"who are you?" question that kills cold PR pitches. The announcement targets the
launch window; timing it to coincide with a culturally relevant moment (harvest
season, summer bank holidays, a food festival week) maximises the natural interest
in the story.

**SEO programme:** County guide articles — "farm shops in Lincolnshire", "honesty boxes
in the Cotswolds", "farm shops near Harrogate" — targeting the long-tail queries that
aggregate to significant volume. Structured data (JSON-LD LocalBusiness markup) on all
953 seed listings from day one. Technical Core Web Vitals compliance at launch is not
optional; it is the mechanism by which Farmmap's listing pages rank above BigBarn's
ageing infrastructure on Google's page-experience signals.

**Target by Month 6:** 40,000 monthly visitors; 50+ claimed listings; PRcoverage in
at least two national or regional outlets.

---

## Phase 2 — Traffic-Triggered Farm Shop Owner Acquisition (Months 3–12)

**Objective:** Convert traffic evidence into Bronze subscribers via warm outreach.
Target 25–45 paying Bronze shops by Month 12.

**Traffic-triggered email:** When a listing's page views cross a threshold (50 views
in 30 days), the owner receives an automated email: "People are finding your farm shop
on Farmmap. [X] people viewed your listing this month." This is the lowest-CAC
acquisition mechanic in the model — it proves value before asking for money, and it
reaches owners at the moment of maximum receptivity (when evidence of consumer
interest is fresh).

**FRA partnership activation:** The Farm Retail Association relationship (viability
gate condition 2) is the highest-leverage Phase 2 investment. An FRA-endorsed
announcement in the FRA member newsletter converts cold outreach to warm referral
at approximately 3× the conversion rate. The pitch to the FRA is not "advertise
Farmmap to your members" — it is "let us show your members that consumers are
actively looking for them online, and we will prove it." The FRA relationship is
pursued from Month 1; Phase 2 is the point at which it should be operational.

**Agricultural show presence:** Rural Lincolnshire (March), the Balmoral Show (May,
Northern Ireland), and smaller county shows provide direct access to the 50–70
demographic that represents Janet and Tom (P3 and P4). A branded stand with
live map demonstrations — showing an owner their own listing — is the highest
conversion touchpoint for the reluctant-digitaliser segment. Shows are targeted
from Month 3 to align with spring/summer show season.

**Target by Month 12:** 25–45 paying Bronze shops; 100+ claimed listings across
the seeded 953; FRA partnership agreement active.

---

## Phase 3 — Bronze Monetisation at Scale (Months 6–18)

**Objective:** Establish Bronze as a self-serve, evidence-led conversion funnel.
Reach 100 paying Bronze subscribers — the gate for multi-tenant engine
generalisation investment.

**90-day trial for first 50 subscribers:** The pricing hypothesis confirms the correct
response to the "why pay when Google is free" objection is not a price reduction but
a proof-of-value window. The first 50 Bronze subscribers receive a 90-day free trial
(equivalent to 3 months × £20 = £60 in deferred revenue). This is funded from the
personal capital injection and the Phase 1 PR investment. At the end of the trial,
the conversion to paid is made with 90 days of dashboard data as the argument — shop
owners who have seen their Farmmap traffic and enquiries are far easier to convert
than those being pitched on hypothetical value.

**Analytics dashboard as conversion lever:** The Bronze analytics dashboard (F22 in
the spec) is not a feature; it is the primary sales argument for Bronze-to-Silver
conversion and for convincing free-tier claimants to upgrade. Every iteration of
the dashboard should be optimised around a single question: "Does this make it easier
for a farm shop owner to see that Farmmap is bringing them new customers?" If the
answer is yes, it converts. If not, it is a cost without return.

**Target by Month 18:** 100 paying Bronze subscribers; 250+ claimed listings;
analytics dashboard live for all Bronze subscribers; 90-day trial programme
complete for first 50 cohort.

---

## Phase 4 — Silver Marketplace + Portfolio Signal (Month 18+)

**Objective:** Launch the Silver marketplace tier; establish TractorMap as the
proven second vertical of the portfolio engine.

**Silver marketplace launch:** Silver tier is enabled for Bronze shops that have
20+ ordering waitlist sign-ups (F6 gate) or have been on Bronze for 6+ months
with demonstrated traffic. The Silver launch is not a public announcement campaign
— it is a targeted upgrade path for Bronze shops whose analytics data already
shows ordering intent. The waitlist subscribers accumulated since V1 launch are
the first Silver customers; their activation emails on the day a shop upgrades
create immediate GMV from day one of each shop's Silver activation.

**TractorMap waitlist landing page:** A single-page waitlist site at tractormap.co.uk
("Farm machinery, parts, and services — mapped across the UK. Coming soon.") captures
the portfolio signal. No investment in the TractorMap build until Farmmap reaches
100 paying Bronze subscribers (the gate from the viability-gate.md conditions).
The waitlist page is low-cost to build and accumulates early demand evidence. When
the engine generalisation investment is approved, TractorMap launches with a
pre-validated demand signal rather than a cold start.

**Target by Month 24:** 30+ paying Silver shops; £15,000+ monthly GMV through
Silver marketplace; TractorMap waitlist at 500+ sign-ups; first Gold shops eligible
(those reaching 3 months on Silver + 50 completed orders by this milestone).
